The new FERMEL 17T Mining Motor Grader is a purpose built mining machine designed for operation in tough conditions and applications found in underground mining excavations. With extensive experience in the field mechanized mining, our engineers understand what is required of a MINING Grader, from a successful applicability perspective, and ruling legislation drivers.

 
OPERATOR CABIN

Our motor grader features a fully enclosed temperature controlled operating cabin. We understand however this is not sufficient, as underground operations have height constraints, that determine a new approach: The complete cab has been given a degree freedom along the vertical axis, allowing the operator to move the complete cabin up and down for a distance of up to 500mm via the appropriate actuator from the comfort if his seat.

This being done whist retaining the FOPS ROPS design compliance of the CABIN Enclosure, again a legislative requirement of machinery used in mines.

 
STRUCTURES, CHASSIS AND FRAMES

The FERMEL motor grader chassis and structures are FEA validated and feature a heavy duty fabricated construction for performance in tough conditions.

The centre articulation, is our standard FERMEL bolt-on design used successfully in most of our machines, with heavy duty sealed spherical bearing pivots, that only require lubrication at the scheduled intervals. Our articulation design bas proven so successful it carries an unlimited three year warranty.

 
 
FERMEL T-BAR RING & MOULDBOARD

The heavy duty T-Bar and Ring are designed for maximum life expectancy, and also carry the additional ballast for optimal mass distribution between front and rear axles. The ring is turned seamlessly by two articulating cylinders, fitted with over-pressurization relief valves to protect these in instances where the blade strikes objects when at it extremities.

The blade is mounted with ease of replacement and intervention in mind, with easily replaced mounting blocks, with multiple shims for ease of adjustment.

 
STEERING

Underground operations entail the machine must be able to negotiate the confines of the excavations tunnels. While in grading mode, only the front axle provides the steering, the machine's turning circle must be reduced further when turning, and on a single joystick, the operator can engage either one or both of the steering functions.

Unique piston design, allows for dampened steering, with no shock load transfer to the chassis, and or frames.
